Some mornings, I oversleep.
The alarm screams, I hit snooze,
and before I know it, an hour’s gone.
My coffee’s cold before I take a sip,
and the day already feels like it’s slipping away.

But I get up anyway, because what else is there to do?
Life doesn’t wait until I feel ready.
It never has.

Keating said, “Seize the day.”
But no one ever tells you that seizing the day
doesn’t always mean doing something big.

It’s not just about standing on desks
or shouting poetry into the wind.
Most of the time, it’s smaller than that.

It’s getting out of bed
when you’d rather stay buried under the covers.
It’s speaking up when your voice wavers.
It’s making a choice—even a tiny one—
when doubt tells you to stand still.

No one has it all figured out.
We try, we fail, we start over.
And maybe that’s the whole point.
Maybe life isn’t about getting it perfect,
but about showing up, however messy or uncertain it feels.

So, I’ll keep going.
I’ll stumble, I’ll second-guess,
I’ll laugh at my own mistakes.
But I won’t wait for the perfect moment,
because that moment doesn’t exist.

I’ll take what I have—right here, right now—
and I’ll do something with it.

Keating was right.
Seize the day, come what may.
